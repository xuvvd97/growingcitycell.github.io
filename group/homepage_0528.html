The Growing City-Cell




The Growing City-Cell shows the upward growth trajectory of cities, the increasing large urban agglomeration patterns , the patterns of emissions over time alongside city-cell growth and the evolving transport networks

CASA 2020 Group3- Group Mini Project: Digital Visualisation
The Growing City-Cell, highlights the similarities of a city to a cell, but also to show cities’ growth over time. It shows the upward growth trajectory of cities (growth), the increasing large urban agglomeration patterns (accumulation of resources / nutrition), the patterns of emissions over time alongside city-cell growth (excretion / waste) and the evolving transport networks (movement)
How does the growing city cell help to explore urban studies?
This study highlights similarities between urban studies and cellular biology and explains different perspective of understanding city. These contributes to revealing the potential of shared insights on cells/cities. This is not a value-judgment on this growth of the city-cell, but providing an expository take on it through thematic visualisations

We are using the lollipop chart to visualise the lengths of railways. We can use buttons to choose different year to find how provinces or cities vary in the same year.
If we choose 2000 and 2018. We can find in 2010, the total lengths in most provinces and cities were less than 3000 kilometers. However, in 2018 most areas’ total lengths became larger than 5000 kilometers. It is a great growth.
We are using the circular bar plot, which is different from the traditional bar plot and displays data in a circle rather than a horizontal way to visualise lengths of expressways among provinces.
Here are the plots in 2008 and 2018. We can find nearly all provinces or cities have extended their expressway’s lines. The length of Shanghai, Hainan, Tanjin, and Beijing increased little. It might because the expressways in Shanghai and Beijing had been already developed before, the total land area in Tianjin is small, and Hainan is an island, which is hard to build expressways to be connected with other cities.
Urban Rail Transit System might include subways and trams.
We can use the box in line chart to choose different provinces or cities and find how one area changed in the different years and compare the lengths and development between them.
Here we chose Hubei Province and Shanghai. We can find Hubei started to develop the system from 2009 and was greatly improved from 2012. Shanghai already had the system in 2006 and the length grew slowly after 2010 because it has been developed. In 2018, Hubei Province covered an area nearly 30 times the size of Shanghai, but its total length was only half that of Shanghai.
